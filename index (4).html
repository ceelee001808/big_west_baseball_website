Inside the model

how a readiness score gets made — and what it can't see

The vote

A Random Forest trained on 27,012 JUCO hitters (min. 75 PA) learns what the 199 confirmed Big West transfers looked like at the plate. Each of its 200 decision trees examines 7 batting stats and votes yes or no.

readiness = yes votes ÷ 200
138 yes → 69 · not a percentile, just the vote
≥ 70 — Elite candidate
55–69 — Strong prospect
40–54 — Developing
< 40 — Below threshold

What the trees weigh

Importances cluster between 12–16% — no single stat dominates. The forest rewards complete offensive profiles, not one loud tool.

AUC-ROC 0.653
pick a random transfer + non-transfer: the model ranks the transfer higher 65% of the time (coin flip = 50%)

Read it honestly

  • Ranks hitters by how closely their bat resembles confirmed Big West transfers
  • Shows true percentiles against all 27,012 qualified JUCO hitters
  • Confirmed transfers show their actual destination school
  • No defense, arm, speed tools, makeup, or academics
  • Can't see transfers outside the Big West — a player now at an SEC school reads as "uncommitted"
  • Hitters only — no pitchers
